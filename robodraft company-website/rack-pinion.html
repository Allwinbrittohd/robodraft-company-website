ETK/ENK Series – Electric Rack and Pinion Actuator

Converting rotary motion into precise, extended linear travel.

ETK/ENK Series Overview

An electric **rack and pinion actuator** is a mechanism used for converting rotary motion into linear motion. It typically consists of a rack (a straight bar with teeth along its length) and a pinion gear (a small gear that meshes with the teeth of the rack). When the pinion gear rotates, it moves the rack linearly along its axis.

The electric aspect refers to the use of an electric motor to drive the pinion gear. By controlling the rotation of the motor, you can precisely control the linear movement of the rack. This makes the ETK/ENK series highly effective for applications requiring very long strokes and high speeds.

Key Features:
  • **Extended Stroke**: Standard stroke up to $\text{5,700 mm}$. Extended stroke can be achieved by connecting rack gear, guideway, and aluminum extrusion components.
  • **Direct Drive**: Direct mount onto rack gear without pulley and belt, effectively reducing power lost and improving efficiency.
  • **Precision**: Smooth and precise movement due to rack and pinion mechanism. Ensures repeatability of $\pm 0.08 \text{ mm}$.
  • **High Dynamics**: Capable of high speed and acceleration, up to $\text{2,010 mm/s}$.
  • **High Payload**: Maximum payload capacity up to $\text{185 Kg}$.
  • **Flexible Design**: **ENK series** is designed for more flexible, lower height applications, offering greater installation adaptability.
Typical Applications:
  • Industrial automation and material handling
  • Robotics (e.g., Cartesian Robots, Gantry systems)
  • Automotive steering systems (Electric Power Steering)
  • Cobots and collaborative robotic systems
  • Wafer handling robots and Scara robots
