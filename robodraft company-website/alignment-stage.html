Alignment Stage - LXY Series

Low-resistance friction design with iron core linear motor for ultra-precise wafer and panel positioning.

LXY Product Overview: Precision Alignment

The **Toyo Alignment Stage (LXY Series)** is a critical component in the semiconductor industry that is used to align and position wafers accurately during the manufacturing process. It is typically used in the front-end of the semiconductor manufacturing process, where wafers are patterned and etched. The alignment stage uses a combination of mechanical and optical techniques to ensure that the wafers are precisely aligned with the lithography equipment. This stage is essential for producing high-quality semiconductor devices.

The LXY series features a **low-resistance friction design** equipped with an **iron core linear motor**. Its appearance is lightweight and includes a **large hollow structure design**. Due to this large hollow design, processing can occur on both sides, and it can be easily placed in an optical inspection machine. Diverse track designs, such as built-in rails, crossed roller guides, and standard linear rails, can be selected to support coreless rails.

Key Alignment Stage Features

Precise Alignment

Achieves ultra-fine positioning crucial for complex semiconductor and display patterning processes.

Large Hollow Design

Facilitates processing on both sides and seamless integration into optical inspection machines.

Multi-Style Guide Rail Design

Offers flexibility with diverse track options including built-in rails, crossed roller guides, and standard linear rails.

Good Dynamic Performance

The iron core linear motor ensures strong dynamic response and smooth motion control.

Precision Geometric Accuracy

Exceptional flatness and orthogonality specifications ensure consistent and reliable performance across the entire travel range.

Applicable Industries

  • Panel Lamination for display manufacturing.
  • High-precision printing applications (e.g., printed electronics).
  • Wafer Positioning in semiconductor lithography and metrology.
  • AOI (Automated Optical Inspection) requiring high-speed, accurate movement.
