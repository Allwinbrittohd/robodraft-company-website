Multi Axis Actuators (Cartesian Robots)

Versatile XY, XYZ Systems for Precision Positioning and Automation Tasks.

XYGT Series – Built-in LM Guide Cartesian Robot

Ideal for precision, speed, and movement in Cartesian coordinates (X, Y, Z).

Overview: Built-in LM Guide Cartesian Robot

Cartesian robots, also known as linear robots, are a type of industrial robot that possess a unique design. Circular motion and interpolation motion are possible when using them with a multi-axis controller. The most common combinations are Gantry type, Arm type, and XY type.

With the help of this multi-axis design like XY, XYZ and/or adding a rotation axis for application is possible. These multi-axis systems change the movement from one dimension to two or three dimensions. The **XYGT series** of cartesian systems uses a built-in guideway ball screw mechanism for its precise movements.

Key Features:
  • **Linear Motion**: They move solely in a straight line, making them ideal for applications that require precision and speed.
  • **Cartesian Coordinates**: Their motion is controlled using Cartesian coordinates ($\text{X, Y, Z}$), allowing for precise positioning and movement.
  • **Robustness**: Cartesian robots are often designed for heavy-duty use and can withstand harsh industrial environments.
  • **Payload Capacity**: They are capable of handling heavy payloads, making them suitable for various industrial applications.

Detailed XYGT Applications

1. CCD Machine Vision Inspection

Automated Optical Inspection (AOI) for PCB using an external CCD camera attached to the Z axis, providing high-speed, detailed examination.

2. PCB Laser Engraving

High speed dual drive laser engraving of LCD panels and other large-sized work pieces, requiring coordinated motion across multiple axes.

3. Pick and Place for Small Sized Pieces

Efficient handling and placement of small parts using a single axis sliding table integrated within the $\text{3-axis}$ linear system.

4. Dispensing for Small Sized Workpieces

A cost-competitive solution for precise dispensing applications, suitable for both on and off-production line use, ensuring accuracy.

XYTH Series – Ball Screw Type Cartesian Robot

Engineered for high precision, accuracy, and robust performance in multi-directional movement.

Overview: Ball Screw Type Cartesian Robot

Multi-axis actuators are electromechanical devices that convert electrical signals into mechanical motion, enabling precise movement in multiple directions or axes. They consist of motors, gears, and linear or rotary mechanisms that work together to provide smooth and accurate motion.

These actuators are ideal for tasks that require simultaneous movement in multiple directions, such as 3D printing, CNC machining, and robotic arm motion control. The **XYTH series** is highly customizable, allowing combinations like $\text{XY, XYZ}$ and/or adding a rotation axis. It can also be configured to use dual $\text{Y-axis}$ or dual $\text{Z-axis}$ for enhanced rigidity and load distribution.

Key Features:
  • **Precision**: High precision and accuracy, achieved through the Ball Screw mechanism.
  • **Design**: Compact design for reduced space requirements in integrated systems.
  • **Performance**: High torque and force capabilities for demanding applications.
  • **Stability**: Low backlash and high stiffness ensuring excellent repeatability.
  • **Environment**: Ability to operate effectively in harsh industrial environments.
  • **Customization**: Highly customizable for specific applications and industries.

Detailed XYTH Applications

1. Pick and Place for Small Sized Pieces

Facilitates efficient pick and place operations for small parts using a 3-axis linear system combined with a single axis sliding table. High precision ensures accurate component handling.

2. Dispensing for Small Sized Workpieces

Provides a precise and reliable platform for dispensing applications, suitable for both dedicated and flexible production lines, leveraging the ball screw's accuracy.

3. Surface Treatment

The highly stable Cartesian system is perfectly suited for surface treatment applications like cleaning, coating, and polishing, requiring smooth, consistent movement.

4. Screw Tightening

Suitable for automated screw and bolt tightening, often integrated with a desktop robot setup for complex assembly tasks where repeatability is crucial.

XYTB Series – Belt Type Cartesian Robot

Cost-effective and high-speed multi-axis motion using a robust belt and pulley system.

Overview: Belt Type Cartesian Robot

Cartesian robots are a design of **2 and 3 axis multi-axis actuators** achieved through the multiple combinations of single axis actuators. This approach maximizes the application possibilities of movement and minimizes the time of assembly while maintaining consistency of appearance.

The **XYTB series** is a combination of two or more belt type actuators. It utilizes a belt and pulley system to convert electrical energy into mechanical motion. This mechanism provides a compact design with high power density, making it suitable for a wide range of applications, including industrial automation, robotics, and medical devices.

Key Features:
  • **Cost-Effective**: Low cost compared to vertical jointed (articulated) robots.
  • **Design**: Ingenious mechanical design resulting in high rigidity for reducing vibration and deformation.
  • **Flexibility**: Allows for multiple combinations of single axis actuators to meet unique path requirements.
  • **Maintenance**: High resistance to dirt and debris, offering reliable operation in less controlled environments.

Detailed XYTB Applications

1. Pick and Place for Small Sized Pieces

Utilizing the high speed of the belt drive, the XYTB system quickly and accurately moves and places small components, improving cycle time in assembly and packaging lines.

2. Dispensing for Small Sized Workpieces

The $\text{XYTB}$ series offers a precise and cost-effective solution for fluid dispensing (e.g., glue, sealant, paste) on small workpieces, ensuring uniform application.

3. Surface Treatment

Its robustness and large work area capability make the Cartesian system suitable for various surface treatment processes like coating, cleaning, and light sanding over extended areas.

4. Screw Tightening

The $\text{XYTB}$ provides the required $\text{X/Y}$ movement for automated screw feeding and tightening processes in assembly, reducing manual labor and ensuring consistent torque.
